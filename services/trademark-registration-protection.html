The Trademark Registration Process

1

Initial Consultation & Strategy

We begin with understanding your business, brand elements, and protection goals. Our experts recommend the best trademark strategy including what to protect, in which classes, and jurisdictions.

2

Comprehensive Trademark Search

Conduct thorough search in Indian Trademark Registry and common law databases to identify potential conflicts. Provide detailed availability report and clearance opinion.

3

Application Preparation & Filing

Prepare complete trademark application with appropriate class selection, clear specimen, and comprehensive description. File electronically with the Trade Marks Registry.

4

Examination & Response

Monitor application status. If examination objections are raised, prepare and file comprehensive response addressing all concerns. Attend hearings if required.

5

Publication & Opposition

Once accepted, your trademark is published in the Trademark Journal for 4 months. Monitor for oppositions and defend if any third party files opposition.

6

Registration & Certificate

After successful completion of all stages, your trademark is registered and registration certificate is issued. Your brand is now legally protected for 10 years (renewable indefinitely).

Frequently Asked Questions

How do I register a trademark in India?

Trademark registration in India involves: 1) Conducting a comprehensive trademark search, 2) Filing the application with the Trade Marks Registry under appropriate class(es), 3) Examination by the Registrar, 4) Publication in the Trademark Journal, 5) Opposition period (4 months), 6) Final registration if no opposition. The process typically takes 12-18 months if there are no objections or opposition.

How long does trademark registration take in India?

The trademark registration process in India typically takes 12-18 months from filing to registration, provided there are no objections, oppositions, or delays. Expedited examination is available for an additional fee, which can reduce the timeline. Factors affecting the timeline include examination objections, opposition from third parties, and completeness of the application.

What are the trademark classes in India?

India follows the Nice Classification system with 45 trademark classes: Classes 1-34 cover goods and Classes 35-45 cover services. Choosing the correct class is crucial for trademark protection. Our experts help identify all relevant classes for your business to ensure comprehensive protection of your brand across all relevant categories.

How much does trademark registration cost in India?

Trademark registration costs in India vary based on the applicant type and number of classes. Government fees range from ₹4,500 (for individuals/startups) to ₹9,000 per class (for others). Professional fees for trademark search, application drafting, filing, and prosecution support are additional. We offer transparent pricing with no hidden costs.

How long is a trademark valid in India?

A registered trademark in India is valid for 10 years from the date of application. It can be renewed indefinitely for successive 10-year periods. Renewal applications should be filed within 6 months before expiry (or within 6 months after expiry with additional fees). We provide trademark renewal reminder services to ensure continuous protection.

Can I trademark a logo or only a name?

You can trademark both names (word marks) and logos (device marks), or a combination of both (composite marks). Each type offers different protection levels. Word marks protect the text regardless of font or styling, while device marks protect the specific design. We help determine the best trademark strategy for your brand based on your business needs.

What happens if someone opposes my trademark application?

If a trademark opposition is filed, you have the right to file a counter-statement within 2 months. The matter then goes through evidence rounds and a hearing before the Registrar. Our experienced attorneys handle all aspects of opposition proceedings, including evidence preparation, legal arguments, and representation at hearings to protect your trademark rights.

Do I need to trademark my business name?

While company name registration and trademark registration are different, registering your business name as a trademark provides exclusive rights to use that name in commerce for your goods/services. This prevents competitors from using confusingly similar names and protects your brand identity. For startups and growing businesses, trademark registration is essential for brand protection and future growth.
